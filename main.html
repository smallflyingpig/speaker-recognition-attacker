Learning to Fool the Speaker Recognition


Jiguo Li Xinfeng Zhang Chuanmin Jia Jizheng Xu Li Zhang Yue Wang Siwei Ma✝ Wen Gao

ICT,CAS ✝PKU UCAS Bytedance

How to fool the speaker recognition model.

Abstract

Due to the widespread deployment of fingerprint/face/speaker recognition systems, attack for deep learning based biometric systems has drawn more and more attention. Previous research mainly studies the attack to the vision-based system, such as fingerprint and face recognition, while the attack for speaker recognition has not been well-studied, although it has been widely used in our daily life. In this paper, we present \textbf{speaker attacker}, a model to fool the deep speaker recognition model by adding imperceptible perturbations onto the raw speech waveform. The speaker attacker is fast because no iteration is needed once the model is trained. We also present an effective method to optimize the speaker attacker to fool the speaker recognition model with minimal perturbations. We attack the state-of-the-art speaker recognition model on the TIMIT dataset and achieve a $92.7\%$ sentence error rate with an average SNR $45\text{dB}$ and PESQ 4.24 with speech rather faster than real-time.
 

Framework

Illustration of our framework. Our speaker recognition attacker is applied to the raw speech input and generates a speech with perturbations, which can fool the following speaker recognition model although the perturbations are imperceptible. Besides, a pretrained phoneme recognition model is used to help to train the attacker network.
